# San Sebastián 🏖️

*Golden sand, green headlands, and the finest small plates on the Iberian coast.*

**Dates:** 17–20 August 2026 (4 days / 3 nights)
**Base:** Kasa Reina, Old Town (Donostia)
**Region:** Basque Country, Spain
**The shape:** A late Monday landing, then two full days in a beach → mountain → pintxos rhythm, and an unhurried final morning before the flight home.

### Day 1 · Mon 17 Aug · Arrival & first night
> You land in the dark and let the Old Town swallow you whole. No plans tonight beyond a cold glass and an early pillow. The feast begins tomorrow.

- **21:00** 🚕 **Land in Bilbao (BIO).** Clear the airport and make for the bus stop out front.
- 🚕 **Transfer to San Sebastián.** PESA / Avanza **line E21**, around 75 min to the centre, €17.10. Buses run hourly until 23:45 (extra summer departures near :15 and :45), so aim for the ~22:15 or ~22:45 and you are golden.
- 🛏️ **Check in at Kasa Reina**, drop the bags, breathe.

> ⭐ **The Move:** if anyone still has legs, slip out for a single glass of **txakoli**, the bone-dry Basque white poured from a height, and one pintxo near the hotel. First taste of the trip. Then sleep.

> 🎯 **Book it:** for a group of 8, two pre-booked shuttles (about €180 each, up to 4) are the painless door-to-door alternative to the bus.

### Day 2 · Tue 18 Aug · La Concha, Monte Urgull & the food tour
> The classic Donostia day. Sea in the morning, a small mountain after lunch, and a guided crawl through the Old Town after dark. Pace yourself. There is a lot of eating ahead.

- ☕ **Slow start.** Wake at leisure, then coffee and a walk the length of the **La Concha promenade**, the white wrought-iron railing that every postcard of this city is built around.
- 🏖️ **La Concha beach.** The bay is calm, shallow, and almost absurdly photogenic. Claim a patch of sand and ease in.
- 🍴 **Lunch at La Cuchara de San Telmo.** A standing-room legend behind the market. Not toothpick-and-counter fare but a tiny kitchen firing out plates like braised veal cheek in red wine. Order two rounds more than you planned.
- **14:00** 🥾 **Monte Urgull.** A gentle 1.5-hour round trip up the headland that closes the bay, crowned by the great statue of Christ watching over the city.
  - 🍸 **Urgulleko Polboriña.** A hidden bar built into an old powder magazine near the summit. Cold drinks, sea breeze, and the best view you will earn all day.
- 🛏️ **Shower and reset** at the hotel.
- **18:30** 👀 Walk to the **Secret Food Tour** meeting point near the **Good Shepherd Cathedral**, the neo-Gothic spire visible from half the city.
- **19:00** 🍴 **Secret Food Tour.** Someone who actually lives here walks you to the counters you would never find alone. Arrive hungry.

> ⭐ **The Move:** that drink at Urgulleko Polboriña. Most visitors climb Urgull and never realise there is a bar at the top with the whole bay laid out below.

> 🎯 **Book it:** reserve the Secret Food Tour well ahead. August slots vanish.

### Day 3 · Wed 19 Aug · Tortilla, Monte Igueldo & the pintxos crawl
> An early tortilla, a funicular up the far headland, an afternoon on the surf beach, then the main event: a proper Old Town pintxos crawl through four of the best counters in Spain.

- **08:00** ☕ **Bar Antonio.** Get there early and claim your spot at the bar.
- **09:00** 🍴 **The tortilla at Bar Antonio.** The reason you set an alarm. Barely set in the middle, oozing, cut into a fat wedge. Locals queue for this. You are ahead of them.
- 🥾 **Monte Igueldo.** Ride the century-old funicular up the headland on the far side of the bay.
  - 👀 **The tower and the amusement park.** A charmingly old-fashioned park perched at the top, and the money shot of La Concha curling out below you.
- 🍴 **Light lunch in Gros.** Cross the river to the cooler, surfier neighbourhood and keep it easy. Tonight is a marathon.
- 🏖️ **Zurriola beach.** Gros's surf beach, rougher and younger than La Concha. Good for a swim and a doze.
- 🛏️ **Shower and change.**
- **20:00** 🍴 **The pintxos crawl.** Four bars, one glorious progression. Order at the counter, stand, eat, move on, repeat.
  1. **Casa Urola.** Start elevated. Their seasonal, plated pintxos are the "we are taking this seriously" opener.
  2. **Ganbara.** The legend, and Anthony Bourdain's pick. Order the wild-mushroom sauté crowned with a runny egg yolk. This is the plate people fly here for.
  3. **Lanperna.** Quieter and seafood-forward. Your palate cleanser between heavyweights.
  4. **La Viña.** You finish on the original burnt Basque cheesecake: torched top, molten centre, the dish a thousand menus now imitate. Order it without discussion.

> ⭐ **The Move:** the mushroom-and-egg at Ganbara. If you eat one thing in San Sebastián, it is this.

> 💡 **Local intel:** start at 20:00 for first crack at the counters. Locals eat later, so the early window is yours.

### Day 4 · Thu 20 Aug · A slow last morning
> One more swim, one more coffee on the promenade, then a well-timed run to the airport. Do not get greedy with the clock.

- 🛏️ **Check out** and leave the bags at the hotel.
- ☕ Coffee and a final walk along the **La Concha promenade**.
- 🏖️ **Ondarreta beach.** The quieter, residential end of the bay under Monte Igueldo. A calm last dip before you go.
- **18:45** 🚕 **Leave for Bilbao Airport** by pre-booked taxi or transfer, the exact-time, door-to-door option for the group.
- **20:00** 🚕 **Arrive at Bilbao Airport.**
- **21:40** ✈️ **Flight departs.**

> ⚠️ **Heads up:** the public E21 bus back runs on the hour and stops at 21:00, so there is no 18:45 bus. If you skip the private transfer, take the 18:00 bus (arrives ~19:15, a safe 2h+ buffer) rather than the 19:00 (arrives ~20:15, tight for the 21:40 flight).
